Historical Places in India

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the south bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, to house the tomb of his favourite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar, officially called the Qutb Minar and also known as the Qutb Minar or Qutab Minar, is a minaret that forms part of the Qutb complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the Mehrauli area of Delhi, India.

Red Fort

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a historic fort complex located in the heart of Delhi, India. It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century and served as the imperial palace of the Mughal Empire for nearly 200 years.

Ajanta Caves

The Ajanta Caves are 30 rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments near Aurangabad, Maharashtra, India. The caves include paintings and sculptures described as among the finest in Indian art, particularly expressive depictions of the Buddha and complex narrative scenes.

Hampi

Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the ruins of the ancient city of Vijayanagara, in the Indian state of Karnataka. It is known for its large number of royal and sacred complexes, as well as its intricate carvings and sculptures.

India Gate

India Gate is a war memorial located near the Kartavya Path on the eastern edge of the "ceremonial axis" of New Delhi, India. It was designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens and built to commemorate the 74,187 soldiers of the Indian Army who died in the First World War and the Third Anglo-Afghan War.
